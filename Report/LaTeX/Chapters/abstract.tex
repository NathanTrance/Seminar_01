\begin{abstract}
Diffusion and flow-based generative models have achieved remarkable sample quality, but their iterative sampling process requires tens to hundreds of neural network evaluations, making real-time deployment prohibitively expensive. This survey reviews the recent paradigm shift toward one-step and few-step generative modeling through the lens of flow matching. We begin with the foundational framework of flow matching, which provides the mathematical backbone for understanding transport maps between noise and data. We then trace the development of modern methods that directly learn the transport map in a single step. We cover four key methodological advances---consistency-based velocity matching, mean-flow training from scratch, scalable continuous-time distillation, and terminal-velocity regularization with Wasserstein guarantees---and discuss their theoretical underpinnings, practical trade-offs, and state-of-the-art empirical results. Finally, we examine extensions to stochastic meta-flow maps that enable efficient reward alignment and inference-time steering without trajectory rollouts. Our unified treatment reveals a coherent progression from simulation-based sampling to direct prediction, and provides practical guidance for researchers entering the field of efficient generative modeling.
\end{abstract}
