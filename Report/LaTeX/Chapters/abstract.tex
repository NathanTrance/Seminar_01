\begin{abstract}
Flow matching has emerged as a powerful simulation-free framework for continuous generative modeling, yet producing a single high-quality sample still requires tens to hundreds of neural function evaluations. This survey traces the rapidly evolving push toward one-step flow matching: the distillation of an entire generative trajectory into a single network call. We begin by reviewing the foundations of flow matching and conditional flow matching, then examine traditional acceleration strategies including cache-based sampling and step distillation. We next discuss consistency models, which provide the conceptual foundation for all velocity-based one-step methods through the unifying mechanism of stop-gradient consistency training. The core of the survey is devoted to four velocity-based one-step families---consistency flow matching, shortcut models, mean flows, and AlphaFlow---analyzing their training objectives, theoretical guarantees, and practical trade-offs. We further summarize experimental setups, standard baselines, and state-of-the-art results, showing that the quality gap between one-step and many-step generation has narrowed substantially. By unifying these developments under a single narrative, we highlight the field's progression from simulation-based sampling to direct prediction of the transport map, and provide practical guidance for researchers entering efficient generative modeling.
\end{abstract}
